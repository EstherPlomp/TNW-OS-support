Open Science
TU Delft
Open Science is the movement to make scientific research and its outputs more accessible and transparently available, allowing a wider range of contributions that will increase research quality. Several concepts belong under the umbrella term of ‘Open Science’ and these include equity and inclusion, Open Data, Open Software, Open Methods, Open Hardware, Open Review, Open Access, FAIR, Open Education, Community building, Citizen Science.

Software Citation
Licenses
Software
Citation
Software citation is important to receive credit for software outputs and to ensure permanent references to the software. While platforms such as GitHub/GitLab/Bitbucket are great for reuse and collaboration, they are no long-term solutions for software preservation. Instead, a snapshot of the software should be shared using a data repository. This data repository will provide you with a persistent identifier, or DOI, which is key for making the code citable.
